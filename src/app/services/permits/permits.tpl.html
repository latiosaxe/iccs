  • Do you Need a Permit to fly a Part 135 or a 121 Aircraft?

    ICCS offers legal representation at General Direction of Civil Aviation “DGAC” to efficiently process and promptly obtain any permit for all types of aircraft.

    One Time Permits

    • Only for aircraft occasionally flying into Mexico

    Overflight Permits

    • All aircraft using Mexican Air Space must apply to get it

    Indefinite Blanket Permits for Passengers, Ambualnce & Cargo Flights

    • FAR Part 135
    • This Permit is applicable for aircraft Smaller than 3.5 tons of payload or less than 14 seats
    • This permit may cover from one aircraft to the whole fleet of a company
    • The permit is Indefinite, however requires a Yearly Verification
    • FAR Part 121
    • This Permit is applicable for aircraft Larger than 3.5 tons of payload or more than 14 seats
    • This permit may cover from one aircraft to the whole fleet of a company
    • The permit is Indefinite. Do not require a Yearly Verifications
    • Flying with this permit requires additionally a Landing Authorization at every flight
